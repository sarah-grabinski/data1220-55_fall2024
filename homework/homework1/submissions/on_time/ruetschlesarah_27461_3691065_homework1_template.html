Question 1

Data Set 1: People from England and Wales above the age of 16, not living in communal homes.

Data Set 2: Records of investigated crimes, compiled with police discretion.

Question 2

Data Set 1: The sampling strategy seems to be stratified random sampling as the population is divided into groups (over/under 16 and living in/not living in a communal home) and then a second sampling method of random sampling is employed within the over 16 and not living in a communal home strata.

Data Set 2: The sampling strategy seems to be simply random sampling from the database as each group in the database population has an equal chance of being included in the final sample and knowing that a case is included in a sample does not provide useful information about which other cases are included.

Question 3

Data Set 1: The 38,000 people who participated in the Crime Survey for England and Wales.

Data Set 2: All crimes investigated as recorded by the police at their discretion.

Question 4

The target population is anyone living in England and Wales, specifically who may have experienced crime.

Question 5

Data Set 1 is complied of self-reported experiences so it’s reliability may be questioned as only those who wanted to respond did and their responses come from the memory of their experiences. Data Set 2 is more reliable as the information was recorded closer to the time of incident, but was recorded at the discretion of the police officers involved. Both data sets would seem valid, Data Set 1 for people’s experiences of crimes and Data Set 2 for reported crimes, but both miss incidents of unreported crimes to the survey or to the police. The conclusions drawn from Data Set 1 can be somewhat generalized to the broader population with the knowledge that it excludes those under 16 and those living in communal homes, as well as those who chose not to respond to the survey. The conclusions drawn from Data Set 2 might be less able to be generalized as they are based only on reported crimes and the information that the police officers deemed relevant.
